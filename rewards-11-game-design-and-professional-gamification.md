# 11. Game Design and Professional Gamification

## Core idea

Gamification is not adding points and badges. Good gamification makes progress, challenge, feedback, and mastery visible.

Professional SaaS gamification should feel credible.

## Useful SaaS mechanics

- Maturity scores
- Health scores
- Setup progress
- Benchmarking
- Certifications
- Best-practice completion
- Risk reduction
- Team milestones
- Workflow quality scores
- Personal bests
- Expert status

## Risky mechanics

- Public leaderboards
- Streaks
- Random rewards
- Childish badges
- Arbitrary points
- Competitive rankings without context

## SaaS example: cybersecurity platform

Bad version:

- Badge for clicking through training.

Better version:

- Security maturity score
- Risk reduction over time
- Benchmarks against similar companies
- Recognition for closing high-risk vulnerabilities
- Certification for audit readiness

## SaaS example: customer-support platform

Bad version:

- Leaderboard for most tickets closed.

Better version:

- First-contact resolution
- Customer satisfaction
- Helpful internal notes
- Knowledge-base contributions
- Fewer repeat issues

## Questions

1. Does the mechanic feel professional?
2. Does it reward mastery or activity?
3. Is progress meaningful?
4. Could competition reduce collaboration?
5. What should be private vs public?

## Activity

Choose a SaaS product and design:

- One progress mechanic
- One mastery mechanic
- One team-recognition mechanic
- One benchmark
- One guardrail

Then explain what mechanics you deliberately avoided and why.
