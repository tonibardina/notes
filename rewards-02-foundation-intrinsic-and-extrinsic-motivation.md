# 2. Foundation: Intrinsic and Extrinsic Motivation

## Summary

Human behavior is shaped by both intrinsic and extrinsic motivation.

**Intrinsic motivation** means people act because the activity itself is meaningful, useful, enjoyable, identity-reinforcing, or satisfying.

**Extrinsic motivation** means people act because of an external reward or consequence.

In SaaS, users are often intrinsically motivated by:

- Doing their job well
- Saving time
- Avoiding mistakes
- Looking competent
- Helping their team
- Getting control over chaos
- Making better decisions
- Reducing uncertainty

They are extrinsically motivated by:

- Discounts
- Credits
- Status
- Certifications
- Manager visibility
- Team recognition
- Access to features
- Renewal or expansion benefits

The best product reward systems support a user's existing motivation rather than replacing it.

## SaaS example

A project-management app gives users confetti every time they create a task.

That may feel fun once, but it does not necessarily help the user succeed. A better reward might show:

- "Your team now has clear owners for all launch tasks."
- "You removed 3 blockers this week."
- "Your project risk dropped from high to medium."

This reward connects to competence and control, not empty activity.

## Questions

1. What does the user already want to accomplish?
2. What emotional state are they seeking: clarity, control, confidence, speed, status, relief, mastery, belonging?
3. Does the reward support the user's own goal?
4. Does the reward make the user more capable or more dependent?
5. Are we rewarding professional progress or product busyness?
