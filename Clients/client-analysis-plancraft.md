# Client Analysis: Plancraft

## Client name

Plancraft

## Summary of relevant conversation context

Plancraft is a perception-risk account: the payout engine was described as stable for reps, but manager workflows and the admin dashboard made the product feel immature. The account risk comes from reliability and UX debt in the control plane, not from the core calculation engine.

## Product-related pain points identified

| Pain point | Pain point category | Type of pain point | Estimated criticality | Supporting evidence or excerpts | Suggested product implications or follow-up actions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Manager workflows and the admin dashboard did not meet the trust bar | Admin UX and workflow reliability | Usability gap | High | "issues... are limited to the five managers’ workflows and the admin dashboard" | Improve manager-facing workflow polish and dashboard clarity before similar enterprise rollouts. |
| A cluster of small workflow defects created a "half baked" perception | Product quality and UX polish | UX debt | High | Internal feedback referenced dropdowns, user management, team target cutoffs, invite/reactivation, and retro team dates as pain points | Group these into a focused quality sprint for admin experience rather than scattering them across backlog items. |
| Lack of clarity outweighed engine flexibility | Explainability and usability | UX gap | High | Competitor comparison concluded Dolfin has flexibility "at the cost of clarity" | Tighten navigation, labels, states, and admin task completion paths. |
| Trust deteriorates quickly when issues feel ignored or delivery feels slow | Reliability perception | Strategic product risk | High | Feedback emphasized that trust may already be broken and speed of improvement matters | Add a visible quality roadmap and measurable improvements for admin surfaces with highest executive exposure. |

## Suggested product implications or follow-up actions

- Treat Plancraft as a warning that control-plane UX can sink an otherwise functional product.
- Prioritize high-visibility admin fixes that reduce the feeling of product immaturity.
- Use this account to define a minimum quality bar for manager workflows before scaling upmarket.
