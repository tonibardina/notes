# Client Analysis: Aufinity

## Client name

Aufinity

## Summary of relevant conversation context

Aufinity is the clearest example of a strategic mismatch rather than a pure product failure. The account churned because new leadership rejected rep-level transparency, but the conversations still surfaced product gaps in approval workflows and usability that matter for similar customers.

## Product-related pain points identified

| Pain point | Pain point category | Type of pain point | Estimated criticality | Supporting evidence or excerpts | Suggested product implications or follow-up actions |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Dolfin lacks more flexible approval routing | Approval workflow | Missing feature | Medium | Jan requested an "OR" step in approval flows; workaround required a dedicated account | Add conditional approval routing for organizations with non-linear signoff structures. |
| Pending approvals are easy to miss | Approval workflow and reminders | Missing feature | Medium | Jan asked for reminders to approvers near pay-run day | Add reminder rules tied to approval deadlines and payout dates. |
| Validation state can be confusing when approval flows are changed | Workflow clarity | UX gap | Medium | Compensations were "not showing as validated although the approval process was technically done" | Improve approval-state explainability and guardrails when admins modify approval structures. |
| First-use comprehension is weaker than it should be | Usability and onboarding | UX gap | Medium | "It’s not necessarily the easiest to understand from day 1" | Tighten onboarding, contextual guidance, and first-value flows for supervisors and admins. |
| Some deals are unwinnable because the buyer rejects transparency itself | ICP and positioning | Strategic mismatch | High strategic impact | "We lost to a belief that transparency in incentives is harmful" | Add qualification criteria and sales battle cards for anti-transparency buyer archetypes. |

## Suggested product implications or follow-up actions

- Do not misclassify this account as a product-only churn.
- Use it to sharpen qualification around transparency philosophy.
- Still address approval flexibility and reminder gaps because those are valid product signals beyond this client.
