# 12. Resource Summary: Hooked

## Core idea

*Hooked* describes habit-forming products using a loop:

1. Trigger
2. Action
3. Variable reward
4. Investment

## SaaS interpretation

SaaS retention is strongest when the product becomes part of a real workflow.

A healthy SaaS habit loop:

- Trigger: weekly business review
- Action: open dashboard
- Reward: clear insight about performance
- Investment: annotate report and share with team

A weak SaaS habit loop:

- Trigger: notification spam
- Action: open app
- Reward: meaningless activity count
- Investment: none

## SaaS examples of investment

- Data imported
- Integrations connected
- Team invited
- Templates created
- Reports saved
- Automations configured
- Historical records accumulated
- Workflows customized
- Permissions set
- Knowledge base improved

## Questions

1. What naturally triggers product usage?
2. What action creates value?
3. What immediate reward follows?
4. What investment makes the next session better?
5. Does the loop serve the user's long-term interest?
