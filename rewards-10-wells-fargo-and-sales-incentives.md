# 10. Case Study: Wells Fargo and Sales Incentives

## Summary

Wells Fargo is a major cautionary example of incentive systems gone wrong. Employees were pressured to hit aggressive cross-selling targets, and the incentive environment contributed to unauthorized account creation and customer harm.

## SaaS/product lesson

The lesson is not limited to banking. Any company can create a similar dynamic if teams are rewarded for the wrong proxy.

SaaS parallels:

- Sales rewarded for bookings, not fit
- Growth rewarded for signups, not activation
- Product rewarded for launches, not outcomes
- Support rewarded for speed, not resolution
- Customer success rewarded for expansion, not value

## Real-life SaaS scenario

A sales team is rewarded for annual contract value. Reps sell advanced enterprise packages to small customers who are not ready. Revenue rises temporarily, but implementation fails, support load rises, and churn increases.

Better sales incentive:

- Good-fit revenue
- Successful implementation
- Customer activation
- Renewal probability
- Honest qualification
- Long-term account health

## Questions

1. What behavior does the official system reward?
2. What behavior does the culture actually reward?
3. What would a desperate employee do to hit target?
4. What early warning signals would appear?
5. What customer harm could result?

## Activity

Design a SaaS sales compensation plan that rewards:

- New revenue
- Customer fit
- Activation within 90 days
- Ethical selling
- Long-term retention

Add anti-gaming protections.
