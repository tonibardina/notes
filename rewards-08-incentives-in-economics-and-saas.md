# 8. Incentives in Economics and SaaS

## Core idea

Incentives shape behavior. But in SaaS, incentives affect multiple groups:

- End users
- Admins
- Buyers
- Champions
- Product teams
- Sales teams
- Customer-success teams
- Support teams
- Partners
- Marketplace contributors

Misalignment between these groups is common.

## Principal-agent problem

The principal-agent problem happens when one party acts on behalf of another but has different incentives.

## SaaS examples

### Sales-led SaaS

Principal: company wants long-term good-fit customers.

Agent: salesperson may be rewarded for closing revenue this quarter.

Risk: bad-fit customers, oversold features, churn later.

Better incentives:

- Revenue
- Retention
- Customer fit
- Implementation success
- Expansion after value
- Low complaint rates

### Admin vs end user

Principal: buyer wants governance and reporting.

Agent/user: employee wants speed and ease.

Risk: product becomes admin-friendly but user-hostile.

Better design:

- Admin controls plus user-friendly workflows
- Governance without excessive friction
- User adoption as a customer-health signal

### Customer success

Principal: customer wants value.

Agent: CSM may be rewarded for expansion.

Risk: expansion before adoption.

Better incentives:

- Renewal health
- Verified outcomes
- Product adoption quality
- Stakeholder satisfaction
- Expansion readiness

## Questions

1. Who is the buyer?
2. Who is the daily user?
3. Who receives the reward?
4. Who does the behavior benefit?
5. Are sales, product, and success incentives aligned?
