# 19. SaaS/Product Scenario Library

## Scenario 1: The onboarding checklist that everyone completes but nobody converts

A B2B analytics tool has high onboarding checklist completion but low trial-to-paid conversion. Users connect sample data, click through steps, and leave.

### Diagnosis

The checklist rewards setup activity, not value discovery.

### Questions

1. What is the real activation moment?
2. Does sample data create real value?
3. What insight would make users return?
4. What step should be removed?
5. What should be rewarded instead?

### Activity

Design a new activation reward sequence:

1. Connect real data.
2. Generate first useful report.
3. Discover one anomaly or opportunity.
4. Share the insight with a teammate.
5. Return to the report next week.

## Scenario 2: The CRM that rewards data entry but not sales judgment

A CRM rewards reps for field completeness. Reps fill fields, but data quality is poor.

### Diagnosis

Completeness is not the same as usefulness.

### Activity

Design a CRM quality score using:

- Completeness
- Freshness
- Forecast accuracy
- Next-step clarity
- Customer-stage consistency
- Manager trust

## Scenario 3: The collaboration tool that increases messages but decreases productivity

A team-chat product optimizes for messages sent. Volume rises, but users feel distracted.

### Diagnosis

Communication volume is not collaboration quality.

### Activity

Design rewards for:

- Decisions made
- Threads resolved
- Fewer unnecessary meetings
- Faster unblock time
- Better documentation

## Scenario 4: The AI SaaS with usage-based pricing anxiety

An AI platform charges per credit. Customers like flexibility but avoid experimentation due to bill anxiety.

### Activity

Design:

- Usage visibility
- Budget alerts
- Experimentation credits
- Team controls
- Efficiency rewards
- Predictable billing options

## Scenario 5: The referral program that attracts bad-fit leads

A company pays for referrals. Lead volume rises, but qualification drops.

### Activity

Redesign the reward trigger around:

- ICP fit
- Activation
- Retention milestone
- Mutual value
- Anti-spam limits

## Scenario 6: The community forum full of shallow answers

A SaaS community gives points for every answer. Users post low-quality replies.

### Activity

Create a better reputation system rewarding:

- Accepted answers
- Durable usefulness
- Expert verification
- Maintenance
- Mentorship

## Scenario 7: The product team that ships features nobody adopts

A company celebrates roadmap velocity. Teams ship many features, but adoption is shallow and support complexity rises.

### Activity

Rewrite incentives around:

- Problem validation
- Adoption quality
- Retention impact
- Support reduction
- Customer-reported value
- Removing unused features

## Scenario 8: The admin dashboard that makes customers feel judged

A SaaS dashboard shows a health score, but admins dislike it because it feels punitive and vague.

### Activity

Turn the score into a coaching system with:

- Clear drivers
- Suggested actions
- Benchmarks
- Progress over time
- Improvement recognition
- Dismissible recommendations
