# 1. The Core Mental Model

## 1.1 What is a reward system?

A reward system is any structure that makes some behaviors feel more valuable, visible, repeatable, or desirable.

In SaaS, rewards can include:

- Progress bars
- Setup completion
- Usage milestones
- Badges or certifications
- Health scores
- Benchmarks
- Status tiers
- Credits
- Discounts
- Referral rewards
- Feature unlocks
- Admin praise
- Team recognition
- Better reports
- More accurate forecasts
- Reduced work
- Confidence
- Control
- Professional reputation

The best SaaS rewards are often not points or badges. They are moments where the user thinks:

> "This product made me better at my job."

## 1.2 The danger

A reward system can accidentally reward the wrong thing.

Examples:

- Rewarding logins instead of completed workflows
- Rewarding messages sent instead of decisions made
- Rewarding dashboards created instead of insights used
- Rewarding seats invited instead of active collaborators
- Rewarding feature launches instead of customer outcomes
- Rewarding sales volume instead of good-fit customers

The key principle:

> Reward meaningful value, not visible activity.
